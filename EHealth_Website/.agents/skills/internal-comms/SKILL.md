## When to Use This Skill
To write internal communications, use this skill for:
- 3P updates (Progress, Plans, Problems)
- Company newsletters
- FAQ responses
- Status reports
- Leadership updates
- Project updates
- Incident reports
- Team announcements

## Communication Templates

### 3P Update (Progress, Plans, Problems)

```markdown
# [Team/Project Name] — Weekly Update
**Date**: [Date]
**Author**: [Name]

## ✅ Progress (What we accomplished)
- [Completed item 1]
- [Completed item 2]
- [Completed item 3]

## 📋 Plans (What's next)
- [Planned item 1]
- [Planned item 2]
- [Planned item 3]

## ⚠️ Problems (Blockers & risks)
- [Blocker/risk 1] — [Mitigation plan]
- [Blocker/risk 2] — [Mitigation plan]

## 📊 Key Metrics
| Metric | Last Week | This Week | Target |
|--------|-----------|-----------|--------|
| [Metric 1] | [Value] | [Value] | [Value] |
```

## Writing Guidelines

### Tone
- **Professional but human**: Avoid corporate jargon
- **Clear and concise**: Lead with the most important info
- **Action-oriented**: End with clear next steps or calls to action

### Structure
- Use headers and bullet points for scannability
- Lead with a summary/TL;DR for longer docs
- Include tables for structured data
- Use status indicators (🟢🟡🔴) for quick visual scanning

### Best Practices
- **Know your audience**: Adjust detail level accordingly
- **Be specific**: "Reduced load time by 40%" beats "improved performance"
- **Include metrics**: Quantify whenever possible
- **Highlight blockers early**: Don't bury problems
- **Propose solutions**: Don't just identify problems — suggest fixes
- **Keep it brief**: Respect the reader's time
